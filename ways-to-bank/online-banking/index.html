Online Security

Online banking is really straightforward to use and our security precautions mean that your money is always kept safe.
We’re using the most advanced security systems to ensure your money and details are kept safe.

  • Secure Code, a one time password using SMS, has been developed. It will be sent to you when you undertake certain transactions in order to verify your identity

  • 128-bit digital certificate from Verisign for encryption of the Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) session

  • Auto-logout if you are inactive for a period or forgot to log out yourself

  • Free internet security software to download means you can rest easy knowing your computer is safe to use.

  • Username and password that you select yourself and a secret question and answer on login to protect you while using online banking.

Online Security

Online banking is built using state-of-the-art technologies that provide a high degree of security. The security infrastructure comprises of firewalls, intrusion detection systems and virus monitoring tools. The security requirements have been implemented and audited by an international consulting firm, using internationally accepted standards and practices.

Online banking uses 128-bit digital certificate from Verisign for encryption of the Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) session. SSL is the industry standard for encrypted communication and ensures that customers interaction with the Bank over the Internet is secure.

In addition to security technology, security is also built into the login process. Online banking enforces the use of a minimum six character password including characters (A-Z, a-z) as well as numbers (0-9). We also use secret question/answers to protect your online banking details.

Log in precautions

Online Banking brings you a lot of convenience! Ensure you don't ever have to regret it by following these simple, easy-to-keep tips!

 Log-in with caution

  • Avoid using shared computers when accessing online banking

  • Avoid logging into Barclays Online Banking or using critical passwords at internet cafes, libraries, and other public sites to avoid the risk of information being copied and re-entered after you leave

  • Change your passwords regularly

  • Contact Barclays immediately if you suspect your Online Banking password has been compromised

  • Use a password on your computer to prevent unauthorised individuals from accessing your information

  • Disable the 'AutoComplete' function within your browser

  • Always remember to log-off online banking and close your browser when you have finished

Never respond to unsolicited emails requesting that you re-validate your personal information or click on a link to a website that requires you to provide your account information. These emails may appear to come from a trusted source, but they are designed to obtain your personal financial information.

Always ensure antivirus software, firewalls and other on-line protection security features are installed on your personal computer and kept up to date.

Avoid downloading software such as screen savers, games and other types of programmes from websites which may contain Trojans or phishing viruses that enable hackers to monitor or steal your personal financial details.

How we protect you when using online banking?

Secure Code

Your online security is our priority, that’s why we have developed Secure Code, a new security feature that helps us to identify that it is really you making the transactions. Secure Code is a unique code that will be sent to your mobile phone via SMS whenever you use certain features on online banking. You will be prompted to enter the Secure Code on the screen before being able to proceed. The activities that will generate a Secure Code are:

  • Registering for online banking

  • Changing a forgotten password or username

  • Addition of beneficiaries to your registered beneficiaries address book

  • One time transactions to unregistered beneficiaries


Virtual Keyboard

The virtual keyboard to log-in is one of the security features available to online banking. With a virtual keyboard, you can enter your password on screen with the help of your mouse rather than your computer keyboard.

Virtual keyboards are designed to reduce the risk of programs that download themselves to your computer and create keystroke logs that can be used to gain access to your accounts. Keeping your convenience in mind, with Barclays Online Banking you can opt wether to enter your password using the Virtual Keyboard or switch it off. From a security perspective, we recommend that you always use Virtual Keyboard when logging in.

Virtual keyboards are an important component in securing your online banking experience. However they do not prevent malicious programs being downloaded to your computer so you should always have an up-to-date virus scanner and firewall on your computer.

Our System Security

Data encryption:

Our online banking service is hosted on a secure, 128-bit encrypted server. This means that any information you send us is encoded for your protection.

Timed logout:

Online banking logs you out automatically if you don't use the service for 10 minutes. This gives you added protection if you forget to log yourself out.

Deactivation of your log-in details:

We'll automatically disable your access to online banking if three incorrect attempts are made to log-in using your details. This is to stop fraudsters making repeated attempts to access your accounts.


FAQs

Account help

How do I register for online banking?

Registration is easy and can be done online in minutes. You will need the following to hand before you register: Passport number, your current or savings account number or your Barclaycard number. Please keep your mobile phone nearby as you will be sent the secure code via an SMS. Once you have these to hand then visit our registration page and follow the on-screen instructions. 

I have forgotten my username, password or secret question answer. What should I do? 

If you cannot remember your username, password or secret question answer, you can retrieve them online via the online banking help section. All you need is your passport number, date of birth, account number and mobile phone to hand.

I have entered my passport number, date of birth and account number to register for online banking, but I am getting an error message.

One of the most common reasons for this error message is because your details that we have on file at Barclays differ from the details you have entered. Please telephone Phone Banking on 04 428 6000 to check whether your passport number, date of birth and account number are up to date. If your details have changed, you will need to update them via the customer maintenance process.

One of the other errors commonly seen is that the account number is not correctly entered. You need to enter the full account number (without the preceding zeros) and this number is typically 5, 6, 7 or 8 digits long. If registering with a credit card you need to enter the last six digits of the card number.

What do I need to login to Online Banking?

To use online banking, you need to ensure that your computer is setup correctly and has the right hardware, operating system and browser. If you don’t have the right set-up, online banking may not work. Here is a checklist of what you’ll need:

Hardware: Any personal computer, laptop or Apple MAC
Operating system: Windows, Vista, Windows XP, Windows 2000, MAC OSX
Web browser: Microsoft Internet Explorer version 6.0 or above, Mozilla Firefox version 3.0 or above, Safari version 4.0 or above, Google Chrome.
You can get the latest version of these browsers by visiting their respective websites
Screen resolution: Our websites are best viewed at a screen resolution of 1024 x 768 pixels. If you want to alter of check your current resolution, please refer to your user manual.


I have logged-in but I can’t see all my accounts. What should I do?

Please telephone phone banking on 04 332 6000 and ensure your passport number, date of birth and mobile phone number are correctly recorded for both your account and card details. If not, you will need to update your details via the customer maintenance process.


Why does online banking sometimes log me out automatically?

If you don’t touch a key or take any action on online banking for more than ten minutes, for security purposes, you will be automatically logged-out of your Online Banking session. You will need to log-on again to continue using this service.

Can I change my password?

Yes. As an added security feature, you will have the option of changing your password as often as you feel it is appropriate. This is done through the ‘My profile’ menu within online banking.

I have a non UAE mobile number will the secure code SMS still reach me?

Yes, the SMS will be sent to non UAE mobile numbers so it will still reach you wherever you are. We have recently been facing issues with Secure Code reaching non UAE mobile numbers as many international telecom providers block messages sent by companies to avoid spamming their customers. If you are not able to receive the Secure Code SMS, we can send you the Secure Code via email instead.

I am a resident of Saudi Arabia – why am I not allowed to register for Online Banking?

The Saudi Arabian Monetary Authority (SAMA) issued a ruling in April 2010 stating that residents of Saudi Arabia should not be provided with electronic banking services from financial institutions operating outside the boundaries of Saudi Arabia. As such, our online banking services have been blocked for customers who are residents of Saudi Arabia.

How do I set-up a standing instruction or recurring payment?

Currently within Online Banking, you can only set-up a standing instruction between your own Barclays accounts in UAE denominations only. You can do this by following the transfer and payments option. If you wish to set-up a standing instruction to another bank you will need to visit us in one of our branches.

Are funds transferred immediately when I use the ‘Transfers’ function?

Transfers within your own accounts, internal fund transfers and credit card payments will be effected immediately. Transfers to another bank in the UAE will be effected within 24 hours and transfers to banks outside of the UAE can take up to 5 working days.

External funds transfers will be effected within 24 hours of placing the request during banking hours which are 08:00 – 17:00 Saturday to Thursday and will be actioned the next working day if placing the request outside of banking hours.

I have transferred funds/made a bill payment online but I want to stop it now.

If you need to pay bills or make payments to friends, family and others, you’ll find it easy to do using online banking. You can immediately cancel standing instructions and future dated transactions at the click of a button. Unfortunately, it is not possible to stop ‘instant’ funds transfers initiated via online banking. 

How do I set-up a standing instruction or recurring payment?

Currently within Online Banking you can only set-up a standing instruction between your own Barclays accounts in UAE denominations only. You can do this by following the transfer and payments option. If you wish to set-up a standing instruction to another bank you will need to visit us at one of our branches.

I am able to view my Barclaycard and/or accounts online but I am not able to make any funds transfer or bill payments.

Please telephone phone banking on 04 428 6000 to check the status of your account and card. Please note that bill payments can only be made from Dirham denominated current or savings accounts, or with your Barclaycard. 

Can I see my account statements online?

 

You can see an online version of your Current, Savings and Barclaycard paper statements through Online Banking. The online versions include all of the information that you currently receive on your paper statements and you can conveniently download it into Adobe (PDF), Excel (XLS) or Quicken Money format.

Paying Bills

What bills can I pay using Online Banking?

You can pay the following utility bills via Online Banking: SALIK, Etisalat (Al Wasl, GSM etc), DEWA, SEWA and ADWEA. You can also pay your own or a friend's Barclaycard bill or transfer money to local or international bank accounts.

How do I pay bills online?

If you need to pay bills or make payments to friends, family and others, you’ll find it easy using online banking. You’ll need to logon to online banking and go to the Transfer & Payments section. You can make payments to registered beneficiaries and also to one-off beneficiaries.

Security

Is it safe to use ‘Online Banking'?

Online Banking is a safe and convenient way to manage your money. There is no reason why you cannot use the Internet to bank online. We use industry standard levels of security to protect you and two factor authentication for all transactions. To ensure no one else can access your account remember to click log-out each time you finish using online banking, ensure your computer is protected, and follow the precautions within our online security section.

What is the Secure Code?

Your online security is our priority so we have developed Secure Code, a security feature that helps us to identify that it is really you making the transaction. Secure Code is a unique code that will be sent to your mobile phone via an SMS or email whenever you use certain features on online banking. You will be prompted to enter the Secure Code on the screen before being able to proceed. The activities that will generate a Secure Code are: registering for online banking; changing a forgotten password or user name; adding beneficiaries; one-time transactions to unregistered beneficiaries.

I am not receiving a the Secure Code. What should I do?

Please check the last 4 digits of your mobile number shown on the Secure Code entry page. If these digits do not correspond to the last 4 digits of your mobile phone number then we have a different mobile number on file. Please telephone Phone Banking on 04 338 6000 to update your mobile phone number.

If the mobile number is correct and you do not receive the Secure Code within three minutes you can generate another Secure Code by clicking on ‘Request to generate another Secure Code’ on screen. If the SMS still does not reach you please contact phone banking for technical support. We can send the Secure Code by email or find another solution.

What is a virtual keyboard and how does it protect me?

A virtual keyboard is a security feature available on Online Banking. With a virtual keyboard, you can enter your password on screen with the help of your mouse rather than using your computer's keyboard.

Virtual keyboards are designed to reduce the risk of programs that download themselves to your computer and create keystroke logs that can be used to gain access to your accounts. Keeping your convenience in mind, with online banking you can opt to enter your password using the virtual keyboard or switch this feature off. From a security perspective, we recommend that you always use virtual keyboard when logging-in.

Virtual keyboards are an important component in securing your online banking experience however they do not prevent malicious programs being downloaded to the computer so you should always have an update to date virus scanner and firewall on your computer.

What if someone else knows my User ID, Password, or Secret Question information?

If you think that someone else knows your password or secret question information then you need to change them immediately. Your password and secret question information are the key to your security as the system matches them to our records to check your identity. Changing your password/secret question information will mean that no one else can access your account, even if they know your user ID. You may want to check your account statement to see if any unauthorized transactions have taken place.
